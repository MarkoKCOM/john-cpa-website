# HeshbonAI -- Brand & Messaging Guide

**Version:** 2.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-07
**Product:** The AI assistant for CPA firms -- automates client follow-up and document collection through WhatsApp.
**Market:** Israeli CPAs and accounting firms.

---

## 1. Brand Identity

### Name Rationale

HeshbonAI combines "Heshbon" (the Hebrew root for accounting/calculation) with "AI" -- signaling exactly what the product does: AI-powered accounting workflow automation. The name was chosen for:

- **Immediate clarity** -- anyone in the Israeli accounting space instantly gets what it does.
- **Cultural resonance** -- rooted in Hebrew, built for the Israeli market.
- **Professional credibility** -- sounds like a serious tool, not a toy or gimmick.

The name is always written as **HeshbonAI** (capital H, capital AI) in all brand materials.

### Positioning

**HeshbonAI is the AI assistant for CPA firms.**

It is not a standalone persona. HeshbonAI powers a firm-branded assistant that acts as an extension of the firm's team. When a client receives a WhatsApp message, it comes from a named assistant representing the firm -- for example, "Maya from Cohen & Co." The assistant identity is configured per firm.

### Tagline Options

1. **"The AI assistant for CPA firms"** -- Core positioning line. Best for website hero and all brand materials.
2. **"Automate client follow-up, document collection, and status tracking through WhatsApp."** -- Descriptive subline. Good for hero sections and landing pages.
3. **"Chase documents, not clients."** -- Reframes the pain point. Strong for ads and LinkedIn.
4. **"Every document. Every deadline. Zero nagging from you."** -- Speaks to the CPA's relief. Works in email campaigns.
5. **"Put document collection on autopilot."** -- Action-oriented. Good for CTAs and landing pages.

### Brand Voice Guidelines

| Attribute | What it means | What it does NOT mean |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Reliable** | Dependable, consistent, trustworthy | Boring, generic, predictable |
| **Clear** | Direct, easy to understand, no jargon | Dumbed down, condescending |
| **Practical** | Focused on outcomes, action-oriented | Dry, purely functional |
| **Professional** | Competent, informed, credible | Stiff, corporate jargon, overly formal |
| **Approachable** | Warm, conversational, human | Slangy, overly casual, jokey |

**Writing rules:**
- Use short sentences. Get to the point.
- Address pain directly -- accountants hate chasing documents. Say it.
- Avoid AI hype ("revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "powered by advanced AI"). HeshbonAI just works.
- Never say "bot" or "chatbot" in any context. HeshbonAI is an assistant.
- No mascot branding. The product is a professional tool, not a character.
- Hebrew copy should match the same tone -- professional but warm, like a competent colleague.

## 2. Visual Direction

### Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| **Primary** | Deep Navy | `#0A1628` | Backgrounds, hero sections, primary surfaces |
| **Surface** | Slate Navy | `#111C30` | Cards, elevated surfaces, secondary backgrounds |
| **Accent Primary** | Electric Blue | `#2D7FF9` | CTAs, links, interactive elements, primary accent |
| **Accent Secondary** | Soft Blue | `#6EA8FE` | Hover states, secondary highlights, softer accents |
| **Text Primary** | Off White | `#E8ECF1` | Body text on dark backgrounds |
| **Text Secondary** | Cool Gray | `#8A95A8` | Labels, captions, secondary text |
| **Border** | Border Gray | `#263247` | Card borders, dividers, subtle separators |
| **Success** | Success Green | `#00D68F` | Success states, completion indicators, positive status |
| **Warning** | Warning Amber | `#FFB020` | Deadline alerts, attention states |
| **Error** | Error Red | `#FF4D4F` | Errors, overdue indicators |

**Rationale:** Dark navy aesthetic inspired by Stripe, Linear, and Notion -- signals "modern, premium tech product" to the Israeli accounting market. The electric blue accent provides strong contrast for CTAs and interactive elements without competing with WhatsApp green.

### Typography

- **Headings:** Inter (Bold/Semibold) -- clean, modern, highly readable. Free on Google Fonts.
- **Body (Latin):** Inter (Regular/Medium) -- same family for consistency.
- **Body (Hebrew):** Heebo (Google Fonts) -- pairs well with Inter, excellent Hebrew readability.
- **Monospace (data/numbers):** JetBrains Mono -- for dashboards, document counts, stats.
- **Scale:** 14px base, 1.5 line height, modular scale (1.25 ratio).

### Logo Concept Brief

The logo should convey: **a reliable, professional tool backed by technology.**

Direction: A clean wordmark "HeshbonAI" with the "AI" portion subtly differentiated (weight, color, or slight separation). No chat bubbles, no robot imagery, no WhatsApp iconography, no mascot. It should look like it belongs on a SaaS product page, not a tech blog.

Monochrome version must work on both dark and light backgrounds.

## 3. Messaging Framework

### Key Messages by Audience

**CPA Firm Owners (decision makers)**
- "Your team spends 40% of their time chasing documents. HeshbonAI eliminates that."
- "HeshbonAI follows up with your clients via WhatsApp so your staff can focus on actual accounting."
- "Onboard in 15 minutes. See results in the first week."
- Pain: staff costs, inefficiency, missed deadlines, client frustration, no visibility.
- Motivator: ROI, scalability, competitive edge, operational control.

**Office Managers (influencers/users)**
- "No more tracking who sent what. HeshbonAI manages the entire document collection workflow."
- "One dashboard. Every client. Every document. Every status."
- Pain: manual tracking, Excel lists, phone call follow-ups, blame when things slip.
- Motivator: less daily friction, clear accountability, looking good to the boss.

**Individual CPAs (end users)**
- "Your clients actually respond to HeshbonAI. You get documents without lifting a finger."
- "Stop being the bad guy who nags. Let HeshbonAI handle the follow-ups."
- Pain: awkward client conversations, time wasted, context switching.
- Motivator: fewer interruptions, better client relationships.

### Elevator Pitches

**10-second (hallway):**
"HeshbonAI is the AI assistant for CPA firms -- it automates client follow-up and document collection through WhatsApp."

**30-second (meeting):**
"Every accounting firm has the same problem: collecting documents from clients is a manual, repetitive time sink. It messages your clients from a WhatsApp number branded to your firm, follows up on a schedule you set, and gives you a real-time dashboard of what's in and what's missing. ### Cold Outreach Email Subject Lines

1. "Your team is still chasing documents manually?"
2. "[Firm Name] -- what if clients sent documents without being asked twice?"
3. "The follow-up problem every CPA firm ignores (until now)"
4. "70% less time on document collection -- here's how"

## 4. WhatsApp Agent Personality

### Assistant Identity

HeshbonAI powers a **firm-branded assistant** -- not a standalone persona. Each firm configures a name for their assistant (e.g., "Maya," "David," "Noa"). The assistant always identifies as being from the firm: "Hi, this is Maya from [Firm Name]."

This approach was chosen for:
- **Trust** -- clients respond to a person from their accountant's office, not a generic AI.
- **Firm ownership** -- the relationship stays with the firm, not with HeshbonAI.
- **Flexibility** -- firms can choose a name that fits their brand and culture.

### Tone & Language

- The assistant writes as a **professional colleague**, not a bot and not a best friend.
- Language: **Hebrew-first** (Israeli market). English available per client preference.
- Messages are short -- 2-3 sentences max. WhatsApp is not email.
- Always identifies who they represent: "Hi, this is [Assistant Name] from [Firm Name]."
- Uses the client's first name. Never generic.
- No emojis in the first message. Minimal use after rapport is established (a single checkmark or thumbs up at most).
- Never threatens. Urgency is expressed through deadlines and consequences, not tone.

### Sample Messages

**Initial Outreach (Hebrew):**
```
Hi [First Name], this is Maya from [Firm Name].
We're preparing your [tax return / monthly reports / annual filing] and need a few documents from you.
I'll send the list shortly -- it should only take a few minutes.
```

**Document Request (Hebrew):**
```
Here's what we still need from you:

1. Bank statements (Jan-Dec 2025)
2. Annual income summary from employer
3. Signed engagement letter

You can send them right here in this chat -- photos or PDFs both work.
```

**Follow-up #1 (3 days later):**
```
Hi [First Name], just checking in.
We're still missing a couple of documents for your file.
Can you send them over this week? Happy to help if anything is unclear.
```

**Follow-up #2 (7 days later, firmer):**
```
Hi [First Name], a quick reminder from [Firm Name].
We need your documents by [date] to meet the filing deadline.
Specifically, we're still waiting for: [list].
Let me know if there's anything holding you up.
```

**Deadline Reminder (urgent):**
```
Hi [First Name], important reminder:
The deadline for [filing type] is [date] -- that's [X] days away.
We're still missing [document]. Without it, we can't file on time.
Please send it today if possible.
```

**Thank You (after receiving docs):**
```
Got it, thank you [First Name].
[Firm Name] will take it from here. We'll reach out if anything else is needed.
```

**All documents received:**
```
Great news -- we have everything we need from you, [First Name].
[Firm Name] is working on your [filing type]. No further action needed from your side.
Thank you for your cooperation.
```

### Behavioral Rules

- Max 3 follow-ups before escalating to the human accountant.
- Minimum 3 days between follow-ups (configurable per firm).
- Never send messages before 08:00 or after 20:00 (client local time).
- If a client says "stop" or "I'll send later," acknowledge and schedule the next follow-up accordingly.
- If a client asks a question the assistant can't answer, respond with: "Good question -- let me check with [Firm Name] and get back to you." Then escalate to a human.

## 5. Social Media Voice

### LinkedIn Tone

- **Professional, opinionated, practical.** HeshbonAI's LinkedIn is not a product feed -- it's a voice in the Israeli accounting-tech space.
- Post from the company account, not a personal brand (initially).
- Mix Hebrew and English posts (60/40 Hebrew-heavy for local market, English for broader fintech reach).
- Keep posts under 150 words. Use line breaks aggressively -- LinkedIn rewards readability.
- No hashtag spam. Max 3 relevant hashtags per post.

### Content Pillars

**1. The Document Problem (40% of content)**
Content about the pain of document collection in accounting. Stats, anecdotes, "day in the life" scenarios. Goal: make accountants feel seen and position HeshbonAI as the obvious fix.
- Example: "The average Israeli CPA firm sends 2,000+ WhatsApp messages per tax season just asking for documents. What if that number was zero?"

**2. Automation Without the Hype (30% of content)**
Practical takes on AI in accounting -- what works, what doesn't, what's overpromised. Position HeshbonAI as the anti-hype AI tool that solves one specific problem extremely well.
- Example: "You don't need AI to do your client's tax return. You need AI to get the documents so you can do the tax return."

**3. Client Relationships (20% of content)**
How better ops lead to better client relationships. The accountant who doesn't nag is the accountant clients love. HeshbonAI lets CPAs be the expert, not the admin.
- Example: "Your clients don't hate you. They hate being asked for the same document three times."

**4. Product & Milestones (10% of content)**
New features, case studies, firm spotlights. Keep it brief and results-focused. No feature dumps.
- Example: "[Firm Name] collected 94% of documents before the deadline this year -- without a single phone call from their team."

## Quick Reference: Brand Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Say "assistant" | Say "bot" or "chatbot" |
| Emphasize time saved and outcomes | Lead with AI/technology features |
| Use real numbers and specific examples | Make vague promises ("transform your practice") |
| Keep copy short and scannable | Write walls of text |
| Show the CPA as the hero, HeshbonAI as the tool | Position HeshbonAI as replacing the accountant |
| Speak Hebrew naturally (not translated English) | Use awkward direct translations |
| Present as firm-branded assistant | Use mascot branding or standalone AI persona |
| Acknowledge that following up is necessary work | Shame accountants for current processes |
| Say "HeshbonAI" | Use AI hype language ("revolutionary," "game-changing") |
